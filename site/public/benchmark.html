The Slop Index

Every frontier model can write. The question is whether it writes like a person, or like AI slop. We ranked 18 of them two ways: mechanically, against real pre-AI human writing, and by human vote (blind, one pair at a time).

How it works

Open method, open data. No LLM judges scoring other LLMs. Every number is a mechanical measurement against writing that provably predates ChatGPT.

Human baselines, pre-Oct-2022

Slop is measured as distance from genuine human writing collected before generative AI existed, so no model can have shaped it: Enron email, a blog-authorship corpus and student essays, archived tweets, and Discord chat.

Five axes, one score

Human vote 40% plus four mechanical axes — conciseness, templating, rhythm, tells — at 15% each. Every axis is put on a 0–100 human-likeness scale (100 = writes like a human) before blending, so each one carries real spread and you can see exactly where a model wins or loses.

Same task, real output

Every model got the identical 112 scenarios across four domains, five samples each, for 19,928 real, unedited generations. Default settings per model (reasoning effort disclosed), no cherry-picking.

Human preference, in parallel

The mechanical index measures slop by rule. The companion game measures it by vote: blind pairwise picks turned into an Elo. Two independent lenses on the same question, and where they agree, the signal is strong.
