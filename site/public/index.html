How it works

No LLM judges. No vibes. Just measurement.

Every number is a mechanical measurement against human writing that provably predates generative AI — plus a live crowd vote. Open method, open data.

01

Pre-AI human baselines

Slop is distance from genuine human writing collected before ChatGPT existed — Enron email, a blog corpus, student essays, archived tweets, Discord chat — so no model could have shaped the yardstick.

02

Five axes, one score

Human vote 40% plus conciseness, templating, rhythm and tells at 15% each. Every axis is put on a 0–100 human-likeness scale before blending.

03

Same task, real output

All 18 models ran the identical 112 scenarios across email, social, essays and chat — five samples each, for 19,928 real, unedited generations.

04

Humans break the ties

The companion arena turns blind pairwise picks into a live Elo. That crowd verdict is 40% of the score and the reason the board keeps moving.
